New Media Arts<\/h1>\n

[\/et_pb_text][et_pb_text _builder_version=”4.7.4″ _module_preset=”default” max_width=”40em”]<\/p>\n

The New Media Arts (NMA) program is a two year AS degree program at Kapi\u2018olani Community College in Honolulu, Hawaii. Our mission is to prepare students for employment in the fields of interface design<\/a><\/em> and animation<\/a><\/em>.<\/p>\n

Earn a Degree\u00a0Having Fun<\/span><\/h1>\n

[\/et_pb_text][et_pb_text _builder_version=”4.7.4″ _module_preset=”default” max_width=”40em” custom_padding=”||0px|||”]<\/p>\n

Wait… what? I can take real college classes learning how to make sweet graphics and cool animations? …and earn a college degree? …and a job after I\u00a0graduate? This sounds too good to be true, but it isn’t. <\/span>We offer\u00a0a 60+ credit Associate of Science (A.S.) degree in either animation or interface design, both of which are super creative, highly technical, and loads\u00a0of\u00a0fun!<\/span><\/p>\n

Animation<\/a><\/h3>\n

The Animation specialization prepares students for careers in 3D computer animation, video game development, and emerging industries employing 3D computer graphics. Topics include the full computer graphics (cg) pipeline, film analysis and storytelling, digital painting, and demo reel development.<\/p>\n

Interface Design<\/a><\/h3>\n

The Interface Design specialization prepares students for careers as interface designers with a focus on web design. Topics include graphic design, typography, graphic symbolism, user interface design, front-end web development, and motion graphic design. Additional courses include digital imaging, design history, and portfolio development.<\/span><\/p>\n

How to Apply<\/h2>\n

[\/et_pb_text][et_pb_text _builder_version=”4.7.4″ _module_preset=”default” max_width=”40em”]<\/p>\n

New Media Arts is a select admissions program. Students seeking admission to New Media Arts begin by taking pre-requisite art courses. An art portfolio, academic transcripts, personal statement and completed pre-requisite coursework are required to be considered for admission to the program. Students are urged to seek academic counseling early and perform well in their pre-requisite courses.\u00a0<\/span><\/div>\n
